Birth History of Yugavitrah

Let us introduce about our service

Inspired by the saying, "Yadi bhujaṅghrau janasya vicārayasi, tadā tvaṁ dharmamārgam prāptaḥ bhaviṣyasi," which means, "When we have the thought to feed a hungry stomach, then we are on the path towards dharma," the story of Yugavitrah begins. As we approached a child searching for food in the garbage, his eyes, filled with hunger and sadness, spoke volumes. His simple yet heartbreaking revelation struck our hearts deeply: "I have no parents to take care of my stomach, and I feed my stomach from those garbage." This moved us to take action.

From that moment, the idea of Yugavitrah was born—a movement dedicated to eradicating hunger and alleviating the suffering of those who have been left to fend for themselves. The name Yugavitrah means "harbingers of change." We, the students of Rathinam Technical Campus, are the driving force behind Yugavitrah, a testament to the power of compassion and the unwavering spirit of humanity with the will to change society and bring about a change that will erase hunger. "We envision a society where every individual has access to the basic necessities of life, where hunger is nothing more than a distant memory."
